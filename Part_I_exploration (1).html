What is the structure of your dataset?¶

This dataset comprises of rows of 183,412 and 15 columns that repersent the bike users. Main variables are the names of start_station and end_station and their latitude and longitude coordinates. Main bike users comprises of two groups 1. Subscriber and 2.Customer, their year of birth (age), and variables is the duration of each trip in seconds.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

The duration of the trips in relation with the genders and user groups. Will the insights provide ways to make a user become a subscriber rather than a customer?

The duration of trip, Genders, Age, Days of the week, and User type.

# Using Functions for the count.
def plotbar(df, var, h='title', normalize=True, rot=0, kind='bar', figsize=(16,6), log=False):
    plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))  
    
    base_color = sb.color_palette()[0]
    ax = sb.countplot(data=df, x =var, color=base_color)

# adding annotations
    n_points = df.shape[0]
    cat_counts = df[var].value_counts()
    locs, labels = plt.xticks() # get the current tick locations and labels

# looping through each pair of locations and labels
    for loc, label in zip(locs, labels):

    # to get the text property for the label to get the correct count
        count = cat_counts[label.get_text()]
        pct_string = '{}'.format(count)

    # for an annotation below the top of the bar
        plt.text(loc, count-8, pct_string, va='top', ha='center', color = 'w', fontsize=12)


    plt.title(h)
    plt.yticks([])
    plt.xlabel(var)

# Showing the plot
    plt.show();

def pieplot (df, var, h='title', normalize=True, rot=0, kind='pie', figsize=(16,6), log=False):
    plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))
    sorted_counts = df[var].value_counts()
    plt.pie(sorted_counts, labels = sorted_counts.index, startangle = 90,
            counterclock = False, autopct='%1.2f%%');
    plt.axis('square')
    plt.title(h, fontsize=15);
